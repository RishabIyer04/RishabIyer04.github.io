NBA 2023-2024 Visualization

In the NBA, there are 5 positions in the game:

Point Guard (PG), Shooting Guard (SG), Small Forward (SF), Power Forward (PF), Center (C)
All these positions play vital roles in the game. However, no matter how productive a player is on the court, as a player ages, they will eventually lose their ability to score more efficiently. However, how much of a factor does age play into a player's PTS (points per game) on the court. That's what this visualization is here to find out.


PG: Age vs PTS

Points Guards have an average height of around 6'2.5". They are the strategic visionaries of the game, helping drive plays to the rim.
This is position is where the most quick and agile players are, driving the game through a combination of elite dribbling, passing, and shooting not only on the 3-point line, but in the paint as well.
Defensively, they are quick enough to get steals from other players to assist their team

SF: Age vs PTS

Small Forwards have an average height from 6'4" to 6'9" and have a very different playstyle compared to Point Guards. They are extremely versatile as they are both agile enough and strong enough to score on the 3 point line as well as the paint.
They are also very important defensively, as they can help with rebounds and covering strong opponents such as Power Forwards.

  • The second scene shows the correlation between Age and scoring production in the form of Points per Game (PTS) for NBA players who play the Small Forward position.
  • The data presented here shows that there is a large amount of Small Forwards within the age range 19-30 than the age range 31-40.
  • However, the interesting part is that the players with the highest PTS are within the age range of 31-40, that being people like Kawhi Leonard and DeMar DeRozan.
