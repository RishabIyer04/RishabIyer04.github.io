NBA 2023-2024 Visualization

In the NBA, there are 5 positions in the game: Point Guard (PG), Shooting Guard (SG), Small Forward (SF), Power Forward (PF), Center (C) All these positions play vital roles in the game. However, no matter how productive a player is on the court, as a player ages, they will eventually lose their ability to score more efficiently. However, how much of a factor does age play into a player's PTS (points per game) on the court. That's what this visualization is here to find out.

PG: Age vs PTS

  • The first scene shows the correlation between Age and scoring production in the form of Points per Game (PTS) for NBA players who play the Point Guard position.
  • The horizontal axis represents the Age of the player, and the PTS represents the Points per game they scored for the regular season.
  • As you can see, there seems to be a bigger variation of data between the age range 19-30 than the age range 31-40.
